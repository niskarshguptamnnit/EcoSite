FLOOD

Flooding injures, kills, displaces people, and causes extensive property damage and infrastructure failure. To survive a flood, move to higher ground, avoid all floodwater, and follow local authorities' guidance. Key survival steps include never walking or driving through flowing water, securing your home by moving valuables and turning off utilities, staying informed through media and official sources, and taking care of health by avoiding contaminated water and seeking medical attention for any illness. How floods affect people Injury and Death: People can be injured or killed by swift-moving floodwater, falling debris, or electrical hazards. Displacement and Loss of Property: Homes and businesses are often damaged or destroyed, leading to grief, sadness, and long-term difficulties in repairing or relocating. Health Risks: Floodwater is often contaminated with sewage, increasing the risk of water-borne illnesses like cholera and typhoid. Infrastructure Disruption: Flooding can damage roads, bridges, power lines, and water systems, disrupting essential services.

HURRICANES OR TORNADO

To protect yourself from a hurricane, you should evacuate if ordered by authorities, but if staying home, move to an interior room on the lowest floor, away from windows and glass. During the storm, do not drive or walk through floodwaters, stay informed by listening to a battery-operated radio, and do not go outside during the calm eye of the storm. After the hurricane, be aware of dangers like downed power lines and unstable structures, and do not return home until it is declared safe by officials.

VOLCANIC ERRUPTION

To protect yourself from a volcanic eruption, prepare an emergency kit and evacuation plan, stay informed about alerts from authorities, and follow evacuation orders if given. During an eruption, avoid areas downwind or in river valleys, shelter indoors by sealing windows and doors to keep out ash, and wear a mask and goggles if you must go outside. After an eruption, avoid driving in heavy ash, protect your skin and eyes, and be cautious of mudflows called lahars.
