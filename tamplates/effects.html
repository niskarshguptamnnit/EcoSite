GLOBAL WARMING

The Earth's average temperature has risen by about 1.2°C (2.2°F) since the late 19th century, with most of this warming occurring in recent decades

BAD AIR QUALITY

Bad air quality significantly increases health data by causing premature deaths, chronic illnesses like heart disease and respiratory issues, and worsening existing conditions such as diabetes and hypertension

MICROPLASTIC IN FOOD CHAIN

A 2016 UN report found over 800 animal species contaminated with plastic, a significant increase from a 1977 review that listed only 247 species.

WATER POLLUTION

Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies by foreign substances like industrial waste, agricultural runoff, and sewage, rendering the water harmful to human health and aquatic life. This pollution harms ecosystems, depletes clean water resources, and causes diseases such as cholera and typhoid. Addressing this global issue requires responsible waste management, the implementation of strict regulations, and widespread public education to protect our essential water resources.

FOREST FIRE

A forest fire is an uncontrolled blaze consuming vegetation in a natural environment, ignited by natural causes like lightning or human activities such as discarded cigarettes or arson. These fires, often exacerbated by dry conditions, can range from ground fires to crown fires, spreading rapidly with high winds and topography. While fire is a natural part of some ecosystems, uncontrolled wildfires pose severe environmental and economic threats, destroying habitats, releasing pollutants contributing to climate change, and causing widespread property damage and community disruption.

DEFORESTATION

Deforestation is the large-scale cutting down and removal of forests for purposes such as agriculture, urbanization, or industry, leading to significant environmental damage including habitat loss, increased carbon emissions, soil erosion, and disruptions to the water cycle. This process severely impacts biodiversity, displacing wildlife and threatening plant species with extinction. To combat this global issue, solutions include planting more trees (afforestation/reforestation), enforcing stricter laws against illegal logging, promoting sustainable land management, and raising public awareness about the vital ecological role of forests.
